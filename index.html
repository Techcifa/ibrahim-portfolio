Why Nigerian Businesses Still Pay Offline — And What's Finally Changing

Walk into any busy market in Lagos or Kano today and you'll notice something that would puzzle a Silicon Valley investor: cash is still king. Vendors collecting payments in envelopes. Finance teams reconciling transactions manually. Business owners spending hours every week on payment administration that should take minutes.

This isn't a technology problem. Nigeria has some of the most sophisticated mobile money infrastructure on the continent. The problem is trust, behaviour, and B2B complexity. Consumer payments digitised quickly because the risk per transaction is low and the habit formed fast. But business payments are different — they involve approval chains, multi-vendor reconciliation, spend controls, and accountability structures that cash has served, imperfectly, for decades.

The shift is happening now for three reasons. First, regulatory pressure from the CBN is making informal payment trails riskier. Second, a new generation of founders who grew up paying for everything on their phones are now running businesses and expecting the same simplicity in B2B contexts. Third, platforms like Duplo are solving the right problem — not just moving money, but giving finance teams control, visibility, and automation in one place.

The businesses that adopt structured payment infrastructure now won't just save time — they'll build the financial credibility that attracts investors, retains vendors, and scales cleanly. Offline payment culture isn't stubbornness. It's a gap waiting for the right product to close it.
